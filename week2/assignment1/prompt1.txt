Payment Processing Time:

Most payment methods (such as bank transfers or credit cards) can take 1–3 business days to process. If you made your payment late in the day or after your bank’s cutoff time, it may not have been processed until the next business day.
Due Date vs. Posting Date:

The due date refers to when the payment must be received and posted to your account—not just when you initiate the payment. If your payment was posted to your account after the due date, the system may automatically apply a late fee.
Time Zone Differences:

If you’re in a different time zone than the billing system, a payment made on your due date might register as late in the billing system’s time zone.
Bank Delays or Errors:

Occasionally, banks or payment processors experience delays that can affect when your payment is received.

Which one works better?

Refined Prompt worked best because it provides clear instructions, structured inputs, and a fixed output format without encouraging unnecessary reasoning. This improves response consistency, reduces token usage, and significantly enhances caching efficiency.

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) exposes intermediate reasoning, which increases token variability and cost, reduces cache hit rates, and is unnecessary for a straightforward billing issue. While CoT can help with complex reasoning tasks, it offers no added value for deterministic customer support scenarios like payment disputes.

Conclusion:
For billing and support use cases, a refined, structured prompt is more efficient, scalable, and production-ready than CoT prompting.
